# Mission: Agnosticism

## Why
To understand agnosticism deeply — not as a vague "I don't know" stance, but as a serious, principled epistemological position with a rich history and real implications for how to live and think about the big questions.

## Success looks like
- Explain clearly what agnosticism is and is not (vs atheism, theism, ignosticism)
- Understand the epistemological argument at its core: the limits of what can be known
- Know Huxley's original framing and how the idea has evolved
- Articulate why this position matters — philosophically, personally, socially

## Constraints
- No particular religious background assumed
- Approach is intellectual, not devotional

## Out of scope
- Conversion or advocacy — this is about understanding, not persuasion
