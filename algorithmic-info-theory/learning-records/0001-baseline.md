One of three new topics started the same session ([[../../quantum-mechanics/learning-records/0001-baseline.md|Quantum Mechanics]], [[../../category-theory/learning-records/0001-baseline.md|Category Theory]]). Unlike the other two, this one is a near-direct continuation of material the user just mastered: [[../../logic/lessons/0015-godel-proof-diagonalization.html|Logic lesson 15]]'s Gödel numbering and diagonal lemma, and lesson 9's Turing/Church/decidability content.

**Implications:** Don't re-explain diagonalization, Turing machines, or decidability from scratch — link back to Logic lessons 9 and 15 and build directly on them. The uncomputability-of-K proof should be framed explicitly as "the same move, again" rather than a new technique.
