Informal fallacy catalog

FallacyPattern
Ad HominemAttacking the arguer instead of the argument
Straw ManRefuting a misrepresented version of the position
Appeal to (Illegitimate) AuthorityCiting irrelevant fame/status instead of relevant expertise
Appeal to Popularity"Many people believe it" as if that made it true
False DilemmaPresenting two options as the only ones when more exist
Slippery SlopeChaining conditionals to an extreme, without supporting each link
Circular ReasoningThe conclusion is quietly smuggled into a premise
Hasty GeneralizationA universal claim (∀x) from far too small a sample
Not ad hominem Flagging a real conflict of interest to weigh testimony is legitimate inductive reasoning. Attacking the person to dismiss the argument's logical content is the fallacy.

Reconstruction procedure

1Find the conclusion first — what is this person trying to get you to believe or do? Not always last.
2Find the premises — everything offered in support, indicator word or not.
3Surface any implicit premise — what addition makes the conclusion actually follow?
4Note linked vs. convergent — does one rebuttal sink everything, or just one leg?

The five-step evaluation process

1Reconstruct — conclusion, premises, implicit premises, linked/convergent.
2Check structural validity — symbolize, check formal fallacies, run the validity test.
3Check informal fallacies — relevance, presumption, weak evidence.
4Assess premise truth — empirical, not logical. What evidence would confirm or deny each one?
5Verdict, in two separate parts — is the structure valid? Are the premises supported? Never blend the two.
