# Mission: Frontier Machine Learning

## Why
To build the deep technical understanding of modern ML needed to work at a frontier AI lab like Anthropic or OpenAI — not just to use models, but to understand how they work, how they're trained, and how to improve them.

## Success looks like
- Explain from first principles how a transformer works — attention, positional encoding, the full forward pass
- Understand RLHF, Constitutional AI, and alignment techniques at a conceptual and practical level
- Read an ML paper (e.g. from arXiv) and understand the core contribution
- Speak confidently about scaling laws, emergent capabilities, and the current frontiers of research
- Be a credible candidate for an ML engineering or research-adjacent role at a frontier lab

## Constraints
- Strong software background (Rust/C), comfortable with code
- Mathematical background TBD — assess as we go
- Goal is understanding, not credentials

## Out of scope
- Classical ML (SVMs, random forests) — relevant only as context
- Production MLOps / model serving (separate topic if needed)
