# Keyboard & Music Theory Resources

## Knowledge

- [musictheory.net](https://www.musictheory.net/lessons)
  Free, structured, browser-based theory lessons from absolute basics. Use for: note names, intervals, scales, chords. High-trust, widely recommended.

- [Adam Neely — YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@AdamNeely)
  Music theory explained with genuine depth and curiosity. Use for: understanding *why* theory works, not just how. Best for intermediate onward.

- [12tone — YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@12tonevideos)
  Deep dives into how specific songs and compositions work. Use for: connecting theory to real music.

- [Piano in 21 Days — Graham Fitch (pianopractice.org)](https://www.pianopractice.org/)
  Evidence-based piano practice methodology. Use for: efficient practice habits, avoiding bad technique.

- [Open Music Theory (openmusictheory.com)](https://openmusictheory.com/)
  Free, open-source university-level theory textbook. Use for: rigorous reference on any theory topic.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/musictheory](https://www.reddit.com/r/musictheory/)
  High-signal, well-moderated. Use for: theory questions, getting feedback on chord progressions.

- [r/piano](https://www.reddit.com/r/piano/)
  Friendly community for all levels. Use for: technique questions, practice advice, sharing progress.

- [r/WeAreTheMusicMakers](https://www.reddit.com/r/WeAreTheMusicMakers/)
  Focused on composition and production. Use for: feedback on original compositions.
