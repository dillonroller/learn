# Mission: Philosophy

## Why
To think more clearly and deeply about the fundamental questions of existence, knowledge, ethics, and reality — and to understand the ideas that have most shaped how humans think about the world.

## Success looks like
- Engage with a philosophical argument and identify its premises, logic, and weaknesses
- Explain the core positions in epistemology, ethics, and metaphysics in plain language
- Know the key thinkers and what made their ideas revolutionary
- Apply philosophical reasoning to everyday decisions and conversations
- Use deontic, epistemic, and transcendental arguments correctly — the formal extensions of logic that classical propositional/predicate logic doesn't cover

## Constraints
- No prior philosophy background, but has now completed the full [[../logic/MISSION.md|Logic]] curriculum (14 lessons: propositional/predicate logic, proofs, soundness/completeness, modal logic, Gödel, fallacies) — comfortable with formal notation and symbolic systems, can move fast through syntax
- Prefers depth over breadth — go deep on the most important ideas

## Out of scope
- Academic philosophy as a career path
- Continental philosophy rabbit holes (Hegel, Derrida) unless they become relevant
