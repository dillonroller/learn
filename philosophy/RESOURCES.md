# Philosophy Resources

## Knowledge

- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Deontic Logic](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/logic-deontic/)
  Authoritative survey of obligation/permission logics, standard deontic logic (SDL), and its well-known paradoxes. Use for: the deontic logic lesson.
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Epistemic Logic](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/logic-epistemic/)
  Formal treatment of knowledge/belief operators, factivity, introspection axioms. Use for: the epistemic logic lesson.
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Transcendental Arguments](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/transcendental-arguments/)
  The definitive academic survey of the argument form, its Kantian origin, and the standard objections. Use for: the transcendental arguments lesson.
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — The Problem of Induction](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/induction-problem/)
  Hume's original argument, in full rigor. Use for: grounding the deductive/inductive distinction this topic inherits from Logic, and Hume's is-ought gap.
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology/)
  Survey of justified true belief, Gettier problems, rationalism vs. empiricism. Use for: the (not-yet-built) core Epistemology lesson.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/askphilosophy](https://www.reddit.com/r/askphilosophy/)
  Well-moderated, academic-leaning, insists on citing primary sources. Use for: checking whether an argument reconstruction is fair to the original philosopher.
- [r/philosophy](https://www.reddit.com/r/philosophy/)
  Larger, more general-audience. Use for: seeing how philosophical arguments get discussed (and misused) outside academia.
