User asked for "a lot more lessons" on probability than Rationality's practical, light-touch scope was ever meant to hold, in the same session as three other new topics ([[../../quantum-mechanics/learning-records/0001-baseline.md|Quantum Mechanics]], [[../../category-theory/learning-records/0001-baseline.md|Category Theory]], [[../../algorithmic-info-theory/learning-records/0001-baseline.md|Algorithmic Information Theory]]). **Implications:** Build real probability theory (spaces, random variables, distributions, eventually decision theory) without re-deriving what Rationality already covered. Strong technical/CS background means definitions and notation can move fast; spend time on the genuinely subtle parts (e.g. why a "random variable" is a deterministic function).
