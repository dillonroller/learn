# Psychology Resources

## Knowledge

- [Book: _Thinking, Fast and Slow_ — Daniel Kahneman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thinking,_Fast_and_Slow)
  Same text cited in [[../rationality/RESOURCES.md|Rationality]]'s resources, but here it's the primary source for dual-process theory itself (System 1/System 2), not just the bias catalog built on top of it. Use for: lesson 1's core framing.
- [Wikipedia — Dual process theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_process_theory)
  Overview of the broader psychological theory Kahneman's book popularized, including its academic history and critiques. Use for: grounding System 1/System 2 as a real research program, not just a pop-science metaphor.
- [Wikipedia — Wason selection task](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wason_selection_task)
  The original 1968 experiment, its ~10% success rate on the abstract version, and the well-documented content effects (the "social contract" version scores far higher). Use for: lesson 1's central experiment.
- [Wikipedia — Belief bias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belief_bias)
  The tendency to judge an argument's validity by how plausible its conclusion sounds, independent of logical structure. Use for: lesson 2, and as the throughline explaining why formal logic training doesn't fully immunize against bad reasoning.
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Cognitive Biases](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/bias-cognitive/)
  Already cited in Rationality's resources — worth returning to here for its treatment of the philosophical debate over how "irrational" heuristic reasoning really is, which sits right at the logic/psychology boundary this topic is about.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/cogsci](https://www.reddit.com/r/cogsci/)
  General cognitive science community — reasoning, perception, language, and the empirical study of mind. Use for: seeing psychology-of-reasoning findings discussed and contested by people closer to the actual research.
