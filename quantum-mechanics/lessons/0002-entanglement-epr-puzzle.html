Entanglement & the EPR Puzzle

Lesson 1 covered one qubit at a time. Put two qubits together and something new happens: most of the states available to the pair can't be described as "qubit A is in some state, and separately, qubit B is in some state." That failure of separability is entanglement — not mysticism, a precise mathematical fact about which vectors in a bigger space can and can't be factored.

Two qubits live in a 4-dimensional space

One qubit lives in a 2D space, spanned by |0⟩ and |1⟩. Two qubits together live in the tensor product of their spaces — 2×2 = 4 dimensions, spanned by |00⟩, |01⟩, |10⟩, |11⟩ (shorthand for |0⟩⊗|0⟩, etc. — "qubit A reads this, qubit B reads that"). A general two-qubit state is:

|ψ⟩ = c₀₀|00⟩ + c₀₁|01⟩ + c₁₀|10⟩ + c₁₁|11⟩

normalized so |c₀₀|² + |c₀₁|² + |c₁₀|² + |c₁₁|² = 1, with the Born rule generalizing exactly as you'd expect — |c₀₁|² is the probability of measuring "A reads 0, B reads 1," and so on.

Product states vs. entangled states

Product state A two-qubit state is a product state if it can be written as |ψ_A⟩ ⊗ |ψ_B⟩ — qubit A's own independent state, tensored with qubit B's own independent state. Example: (|0⟩+|1⟩)/√2 ⊗ |0⟩ = (|00⟩+|10⟩)/√2. Here A and B are just two separate, ordinary qubits sitting next to each other — nothing new.
The Bell state — provably not a product state |Φ⁺⟩ = (|00⟩ + |11⟩)/√2. Suppose, for contradiction, this factored as (α|0⟩+β|1⟩) ⊗ (γ|0⟩+δ|1⟩) = αγ|00⟩ + αδ|01⟩ + βγ|10⟩ + βδ|11⟩. Matching coefficients against |Φ⁺⟩ requires αδ = 0 and βγ = 0 (no |01⟩ or |10⟩ terms), but also αγ ≠ 0 and βδ ≠ 0 (both |00⟩ and |11⟩ present). If αδ=0, either α=0 or δ=0 — but α=0 kills αγ≠0, and δ=0 kills βδ≠0. Either way, contradiction. No choice of α, β, γ, δ works — this state genuinely cannot be split into "A's state" and "B's state." That's what entanglement is: a proof of non-factorizability, not a metaphor.

What entanglement predicts — and the puzzle it creates

Measure qubit A of |Φ⁺⟩ alone. By the Born rule applied to the |00⟩ and |11⟩ terms, A reads 0 with probability 1/2, 1 with probability 1/2 — a fair coin, same as any single qubit in superposition. Nothing unusual yet. Here's the part that isn't: whichever way A comes out, B is now guaranteed to match it, with certainty, the instant A is measured — even if B is a galaxy away and no signal has had time to travel between them.

The EPR worry (1935) Einstein, Podolsky, and Rosen took this correlation as evidence that quantum mechanics must be incomplete: surely, they argued, each qubit already had a definite value all along — the "spooky" instant correlation is really just two matching values fixed at creation, like two gloves mailed to separate cities in sealed boxes; opening one box tells you the other's contents instantly, but nothing mysterious happened at a distance, because the values were always already there. This is called a local hidden-variable account, and it's the natural, common-sense reading of the correlation. Lesson 3 covers Bell's theorem — the result that actually rules this account out experimentally, which is far from obvious from the math alone.

What entanglement does not let you do

Before citing entanglement for "instant communication" A hugely common misuse: "entanglement lets you send information instantly." It doesn't, and this is provable from the same math above, not an extra physical law bolted on. Alice's own measurement outcomes, looked at by themselves, are always a fair 50/50 coin — regardless of whether Bob has measured his qubit yet, regardless of what basis he measures in, regardless of anything Bob does. Alice cannot look at her own results alone and learn anything about Bob's actions. The correlation is only visible when the two lists of results are later compared side by side — which requires an ordinary, slower-than-light channel (a phone call, a message) to bring the lists together. This is the no-communication theorem, and it's why entanglement, despite the instant correlation, never lets you violate relativity's speed limit on sending actual information.

Practice before next lesson

Take the state |ψ⟩ = (|00⟩ + |01⟩ + |10⟩ + |11⟩)/2. Try to factor it as (α|0⟩+β|1⟩) ⊗ (γ|0⟩+δ|1⟩) the way this lesson factored a product state — find actual values of α, β, γ, δ that work (hint: try α=β=γ=δ=1/√2). Then take |ψ'⟩ = (|00⟩ + |01⟩ + |10⟩ − |11⟩)/2 and try the same thing — see where the attempt breaks down, the same way the lesson's Bell-state proof did. One of these is a product state and one is entangled; find out which by actually doing the algebra, not by guessing.
