# Mission: Rationality

## Why
To actually reason better in the moments that matter most — arguments with people close to you, judgment calls under uncertainty, everyday decisions — not just to spot fallacies in someone else's argument. The [[../logic/MISSION.md|Logic]] topic built the skill of evaluating an argument's structure; this topic builds the skill of catching your own mind's shortcuts in real time and reasoning well under genuine uncertainty, especially in personal relationships where being "technically right" often isn't the point.

## Success looks like
- Recognize common cognitive biases (confirmation bias, motivated reasoning, fundamental attribution error, availability heuristic) in your own thinking while it's happening, not just in hindsight
- Reason about uncertain claims using base rates and Bayesian updating instead of gut-level confidence
- Apply the principle of charity / steelmanning to a disagreement, especially with someone you're close to, before responding to their weakest possible reading
- Tell the difference between a disagreement that's about facts (resolvable with evidence) and one that's about values or feelings (not resolvable that way) — and stop treating the second kind like the first

The core 3-lesson curriculum above is complete. As of 2026-07-19 the mission entered a **mastery phase** alongside [[../logic/MISSION.md|Logic]]'s — the user wants to go deeper on "confidence" specifically, building on lesson 2's calibration section rather than leaving it at one paragraph. Phase 2 success looks like:
- Turn a stated confidence ("I'm 80% sure") into an actual expected-value calculation for a real decision, not just a verbal hedge
- Explain calibration quantitatively — track a prediction's outcome against its stated probability, and understand the Brier score as a way of scoring calibration, not just accuracy
- Recognize when a decision is worth formalizing with expected value vs. when gut judgment is good enough — i.e., know the boundary of when this toolkit actually helps

## Constraints
- No prior background in psychology or probability theory, but strong technical/CS background and has completed the full 14-lesson [[../logic/MISSION.md|Logic]] curriculum — comfortable with formal notation, can move fast through Bayes' theorem's math
- Self-study with an AI teacher
- Mastery-phase lessons should number onward from 0004 in the same `lessons/` directory — this is a deeper pass on the same topic, not a new one

## Out of scope
- Full formal decision theory / utility theory / game theory research — mastery phase covers expected value and calibration well enough to use them, not a graduate decision-theory course
- Therapy-adjacent emotional regulation content — this topic is about reasoning better, not processing emotions (that's a different kind of help)
- Centering examples on religion or politics — same secular constraint as [[../logic/NOTES.md|Logic]], examples should stay in relationships/work/consumer-decision territory
