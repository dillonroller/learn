# Rationality Resources

## Knowledge

- [Book: _Thinking, Fast and Slow_ — Daniel Kahneman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thinking,_Fast_and_Slow)
  The foundational popular text on System 1/System 2 thinking and the heuristics-and-biases research program (Kahneman & Tversky). Use for: the cognitive biases lesson — nearly every bias taught traces back to this research.
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Cognitive Biases](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/bias-cognitive/)
  Rigorous philosophical treatment of what a cognitive bias actually is, and the debate over how "irrational" they really are. Use for: precise definitions when Kahneman's popular framing moves too fast.
- [Better Explained — An Intuitive (and Short) Explanation of Bayes' Theorem](https://betterexplained.com/articles/an-intuitive-and-short-explanation-of-bayes-theorem/)
  Clear, example-driven walkthrough of Bayes' theorem without heavy notation first. Use for: building intuition before the formal version in the probabilistic reasoning lesson.
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Bayesian Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-bayesian/)
  Rigorous treatment of belief updating as probability. Use for: the formal backbone of the probabilistic reasoning lesson, and its link to [[../philosophy/lessons/0011-epistemic-logic.html|epistemic logic]].
- [Book: _The Scout Mindset_ — Julia Galef](https://juliagalef.com/scout-mindset/)
  The clearest modern treatment of charitable interpretation, steelmanning, and "soldier vs. scout" reasoning — written specifically about applying this to relationships and everyday disagreement. Use for: the charitable interpretation / steelmanning lesson, the most directly mission-relevant resource in this whole list.
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Base Rate Fallacy](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/base-rate-fallacy/) *(if unavailable, see the base-rate section of Bayesian Epistemology above)*
  Use for: worked examples of base-rate neglect distinct from Kahneman's.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/changemyview](https://www.reddit.com/r/changemyview/)
  A community explicitly structured around steelmanning and updating your view in response to good arguments, with strong moderation against bad-faith engagement. Use for: practicing charitable interpretation on live disagreements.
- [r/slatestarcodex](https://www.reddit.com/r/slatestarcodex/)
  High-signal community focused on rationality, forecasting, and calibrated belief. Use for: seeing Bayesian reasoning applied to real, messy, current questions.

## Gaps
