Practical Rationality Cheat Sheet

The compressed version of the whole Rationality topic — the one to reread right before a disagreement, not just after.

Cognitive biases catalog

BiasPattern
Confirmation biasNoticing evidence that fits what you already believe, missing what doesn't
Motivated reasoningWorking backward from a conclusion you want to be true
Fundamental attribution errorCharacter for their behavior, circumstance for your own
Availability heuristicVivid, memorable examples feel more frequent than they really are

Probabilistic reasoning

ConceptTakeaway
Bayes' theoremP(H|E) = P(E|H) × P(H) / P(E) — update belief by evidence AND prior, not evidence alone
Base rate neglectIgnoring how rare something was beforehand; the classic test-accuracy trap
Proportional updatingShift confidence by an amount matched to the evidence's real strength — never snap to 0% or 100%
Calibration"80% sure" should be right ~80% of the time — check your own track record

Charitable interpretation

ConceptTakeaway
Principle of charityInterpret ambiguous statements in their strongest, most defensible form
SteelmanningThe deliberate opposite of a straw man — strengthen a position before responding to it
Facts vs. valuesFacts are resolvable with evidence; values/feelings aren't — know which fight you're in
The one-line technique "So what I'm hearing is [steelmanned version] — is that right?" — before responding to anything charged.
